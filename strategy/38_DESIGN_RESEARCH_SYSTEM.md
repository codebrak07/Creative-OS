# 38_DESIGN_RESEARCH_SYSTEM — Pre-Design Research Methodology

## 1. Why This Exists & Why It Works
Most design research consists of saving screenshots of popular sites on Dribbble. This leads to copying competitor visual patterns and creates generic layouts. This document establishes guidelines for strategic pre-design research. It works by mapping competitor visual territories and identifying clear design opportunities.

---

## 2. Research Steps & Visual Mapping

```
                         [ Competitive Audit ]
                (Analyze competitor design failures)
                                  │
                                  ▼
                     [ Visual Territory Mapping ]
             (Identify overused layout patterns to avoid)
                                  │
                                  ▼
                   [ Creative Opportunity Blueprint ]
                (Define unique visual/story directions)
```

### Phase 1: Competitor Design Audits
Do not evaluate only visual styles. Analyze competitors to identify their design failures:
* **The Saturated State**: Competitor features overlapping gradient glows, identical rounded card farms, and standard hero text layout patterns.
* **The Copy State**: Competitor copywriting reads like generic AI-generated copy.
* **The Interaction Failure**: Competitor scroll behaviors feel laggy or override native scroll speeds.

### Phase 2: Visual Territory Mapping
Plot competitors on a 2x2 grid representing:
* **Horizontal Axis**: Minimalist vs. Highly Decorated
* **Vertical Axis**: Technical/Informational vs. Editorial/Atmospheric
* Identify empty sectors of the grid to define your unique visual territory.

---

## 3. Pre-Design Checklists & Audits
* **Competitor Checklist**:
  * Identify what font styles competitors use (90% use standard sans-serif).
  * Identify competitor color choices.
  * Identify layout structures (90% use bento grids).
* **Brand Audit**: Confirm the client's current assets to check if a brand strategy update is needed first.

---

## 4. Workflows & Inspiration Systems
1. **Source Outside Web Design**: Look for inspiration in printed editorial magazines, fine art catalogs, historical typography books, architecture blueprints, and industrial design layouts. Do not source ideas from web design templates.
2. **Abstract Visual Layouts**: Recreate layout grid hierarchies using raw boxes and placeholder texts first. Test the layout rhythm before selecting fonts or colors.

## Benchmark Traceability

### Basic Agency
- [INFERRED] Basic Agency → mood board visual teardowns → Design Research System → Analyze three competitor layouts before styling is defined.

### Instrument
- [DIRECT] Instrument → story blocks metrics → Design Research System → Record client data outcomes in competitor audits.
