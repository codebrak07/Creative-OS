# 15_BRAND_STRATEGY_OS — Brand Archetype Mapping

## 1. Why This Exists & Why It Works
Standard corporate positioning relies on generic terms ("fastest," "easiest," "cheapest") which strip brands of personality. This document establishes guidelines for brand strategy using archetypes and visual themes. It works by aligning the product's values with recognized cultural motifs, building consumer trust.

---

## 2. Core Archetype Alignments
1. **The Sovereign (Prestige & Command)**:
   * *Aesthetic*: Ultraminimal, serif typography, high-contrast black/white, vast spaces.
   * *Target*: Luxury brands, premium consulting, enterprise platforms.
2. **The Creator (Innovation & Detail)**:
   * *Aesthetic*: Monospace details, crisp grids, high focal clarity.
   * *Target*: Dev tools, architecture firms, design systems.
3. **The Sage (Insight & Clarity)**:
   * *Aesthetic*: Warm editorial, quiet sans-serif, wide reader layouts.
   * *Target*: Research firms, media sites, educational spaces.

---

## 3. Brand Differentiation Checklist
* **Moat Mapping**: Identify the single engineering or product feature that competitors cannot duplicate, and center all visual assets on this feature.
* **Avoid Neutrality**: The brand must actively select a direction and reject compromising details. Settle on a clear tone: either premium, minimalist, or highly technical.

---

## 4. Brand Positioning Framework

```
                       [ Sovereign Archetype ]
                    (High Prestige, Monochromatic)
                                  │
                                  ▼
                     [ Primary Differentiator ]
                  (Code or Product Visual Highlight)
                                  │
                                  ▼
                    [ System Styling Constants ]
                (Garamoond Serif + Obsidian Canvas)
```

---

## 5. When to Use & Avoid
* **When to Use**: Brand positioning workshops, copy definitions, logo marks, and visual design assets.
* **When to Avoid**: Inline application mechanics, utility views, and simple tables.
