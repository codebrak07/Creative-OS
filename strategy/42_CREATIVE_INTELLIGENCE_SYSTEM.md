# 42_CREATIVE_INTELLIGENCE_SYSTEM — Pre-Design Strategic Intelligence

## 1. Why This Exists & Why It Works
Designers often jump directly to layouts without understanding the business problem. This leads to design debt and misaligned client expectations. This document establishes guidelines for strategic decision-making. It works by setting clear frameworks for question design, validating assumptions, and establishing moats before creative work begins.

---

## 2. Elite Agency Questioning Frameworks
Before choosing typography, grids, or visual direction, answer these three strategic questions:
1. **The Moat Question**: "What proprietary technology, workflow, or positioning does the company have that cannot be easily copied?"
2. **The Sacrifice Question**: "What customer segments, design trends, and features are we actively rejecting to build brand trust with our target audience?"
3. **The Price Question**: "How does the layout structure justify the product's premium price point?"

### Risk Analysis System
Evaluate every visual asset against the visual risk checklist:
* **The Copycat Risk**: Does the visual look like a standard competitor template?
* **The Trend Risk**: Will this color choice or scroll transition feel outdated in 24 months?
* **The Clarity Risk**: Does the storytelling layout compromise information legibility?

---

## 4. Workflows & Prioritization
* **The 80/20 Creative Focus**: Allocate 80% of design time to the core hero container and the primary visual asset. Keep secondary informational views minimal and typography-driven.
* **Reject Symmetrical Choices**: If two layout directions perform equally, select the asymmetric, editorial option to drive brand differentiation.
