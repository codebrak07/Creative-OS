# 21_AWWWARDS_EVALUATION_SYSTEM — Award-Readiness Scorecard

## 1. Why This Exists & Why It Works
Elite digital products must compete at the highest level of global design recognition. This document establishes guidelines for evaluating project builds against Awwwards scoring criteria. It works by setting clear benchmarks for Design, Creativity, Usability, Content, and Innovation, identifying specific areas for improvement before release.

---

## 2. Awwwards Evaluation Matrix (Score 1-10)

### Design (Weight: 40%)
* *10/10*: Exceptional typography discipline, asymmetrical layout balance, and highly curated HSL color choices.
* *Below 7/10*: Uses standard SaaS layout templates, glow borders, or poor typography spacing.

### Creativity (Weight: 20%)
* *10/10*: Novel visual transitions, unique asset placements, and original narrative structures.
* *Below 7/10*: Relies on Dribbble design trends and card-based layouts.

### Usability (Weight: 20%)
* *10/10*: Instant page transitions, WCAG AA contrast compliance, and full keyboard navigation controls.
* *Below 7/10*: Interactive triggers fail accessibility audits or experience lag on mobile viewports.

### Content & Innovation (Weight: 20%)
* *10/10*: Clear, editorial copywriting and creative engineering integrations (like lightweight WebGL or custom canvas details).
* *Below 7/10*: Features generic AI-generated copy and standard HTML grids.

---

## 3. Evaluation Feedback Blueprint
During the review phase, the reviewer must provide a detailed scorecard containing:
* **Current Score**: (e.g. Design 8.5, Usability 9.0)
* **Visual Criticism**: Identify specific layout drift or typography alignment errors.
* **Alternative Approaches**: Suggest 2-3 specific visual solutions to elevate the design.
