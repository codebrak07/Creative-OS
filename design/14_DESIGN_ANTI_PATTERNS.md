# 14_DESIGN_ANTI_PATTERNS — Rejecting Aesthetic Clichés

## 1. Why This Exists & Why It Works
AI builders and Dribbble trends have normalized several visual tricks that create a false perception of design, but dilute actual brand value. This document registers these patterns as failures. Banishing these clichés ensures that every generated output looks like a custom digital piece rather than a pre-packaged template.

---

## 2. Catalog of Banned Clichés

### Anti-Pattern 1: The SaaS Bento Grid
* **The Cliché**: Filling the screen with rounded cards containing icons, small mockups, and text labels.
* **Why it Fails**: It lacks visual hierarchy and visual tension. It forces equal importance on all elements, leading to a boring, dashboard-first layout.
* **The Solution**: Use editorial grid structures with wide margins and asymmetric balance. Let key sections stretch across columns.

### Anti-Pattern 2: The Neon Glow Border
* **The Cliché**: Adding subtle glowing linear-gradients around card elements and button borders.
* **Why it Fails**: Overused in Web3 and SaaS templates. It immediately signals generic startup styling.
* **The Solution**: Use sharp, solid border offsets or high-contrast negative space instead of glow highlights.

### Anti-Pattern 3: Emoji Graphics & Generic 3D Assets
* **The Cliché**: Placing glossy 3D spheres or emojis next to typography headers.
* **Why it Fails**: Childish, placeholder feel. Signals low-budget template design.
* **The Solution**: High-contrast, desaturated photography or abstract geometric vectors.

---

## 3. Comparative Solutions Table

| Cliché Pattern | Why Elite Agencies Reject It | Approved Alternative |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Card Farms** | No focal structure or hierarchy | Open editorial grids, large display type |
| **Heavy Linear Gradients** | Muddy colors, template aesthetic | Monochromatic HSL space, high contrast contrast |
| **Scrolljacking Loops** | Fights browser native behaviors | Asymmetric scroll pacing & choreography |
| **Glassmorphism Panels** | Blurs details, hard to read | Sharp, solid high-contrast canvas fills |

---

## 4. When to Review
Use this anti-pattern catalog as a check-list during the QA audit process. If a layout features any elements from the checklist, reject the build immediately.
